The Reality

Teen Driving Risks

Motor vehicle crashes are the leading cause of death for U.S. teenagers. Understanding the risks is the first step toward prevention.

A Preventable Crisis

Every day, 8 teenagers lose their lives in vehicle crashes in the United States. Teen drivers ages 16-19 have a fatal crash rate nearly three times as high as drivers ages 20 and older per mile driven.

Through Dragons Drive Safe, we aim to educate at least 300 teens and reduce teen crashes in the Southlake area by 20%. Our comprehensive approach combines research, direct outreach, and community partnerships to create lasting change.

Sources: CDC, NHTSA, WHO

Why Safe Driving Matters

Teen drivers are the most at-risk group on the road. Inexperience combined with risk-taking behaviors creates a dangerous combination. The good news: most teen crashes are preventable through education, awareness, and committed safe driving practices.
